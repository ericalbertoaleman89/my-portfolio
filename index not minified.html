project crux

case study topic

Organization and resource management are the most important parts of any project. This goes doubly in the tech industry, which is riddled with complex folder structures and time constraints.

the challenge

unifying orginazation methods

As you can see here, time management is a fractured process, but its components are all interconnected. For example, rebooting a PC means also taking time again to lay out windows, dig for files and reinitiate programs. Staying on track with a project is a time-consuming process in and of itself. My aim with this software is to employ human spatial awareness in streamlining tasks such as viewport layout, sorting, scheduling and goal setting for projects.

  • Date: March 2015
  • Category: UX design for project managment app

review of existing software

Clear app

The Clear app makes your paper “to do” list obsolete with its nifty alarm feature. It is also easy to use and look at. However, it is not designed to help with project scope or resource management.

axosoft

Axosoft’s app is awesome because it facilitates scrum. However, it does not provide the ability to organize and store files, and it is not as user friendly as the Clear app due to list-style menus that crowd the interface.

user profile

Alex is your average tech industry designer: age 32, male, makes $60,000 per year, spends most of his time creating content for games and software. Alex is bright, confident, and organized, with clear goals for project completion. However, somehow his projects still become a labyrinth of folders and disjointed notes. Due to the creative nature of his work, many of those notes cannot be easily categorized and must be bound to a context to make sense.

the design

Why it looks the way it does.

My design is focused on browsing, labeling and placing large amounts of content, so I created an infinite space that the user can pan and zoom within. I used a skeuomorphic design for the header to make a clear distinction between the content in the viewport and the main app controls.

I took a cue from the Clear app and used Apple products as a reference, as their aesthetics are among the most popular in the tech world. My final design is reminiscent of iTunes: familiar and polished.

As a main interface color, I chose purple to evoke creativity, as this app is geared toward the production artists of the industry.

Much like the Axosoft design, this app uses the concept of scrum. However, the UI has been simplified by replacing words with recognizable icons, removing the need to clutter the screen with verbal menus. I included a legend for the icons that can be accessed via the question mark located on the bottom left of the viewport.
